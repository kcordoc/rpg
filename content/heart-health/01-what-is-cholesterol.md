# What is Cholesterol?

Cholesterol is a waxy, fat-like substance found in every cell of your body. Despite its bad reputation, cholesterol is actually essential for life. Your body needs it to build cells, make vitamins, and produce hormones.

## Where Does Cholesterol Come From?

Here's the surprising part: your liver produces about 80% of the cholesterol in your body. Only about 20% comes from the food you eat. This means that even if you ate zero cholesterol, your body would still make plenty of it.

The liver is like a cholesterol factory that runs 24/7. It adjusts production based on how much cholesterol you eat — when you eat more, it produces less, and vice versa. But this regulation isn't perfect, which is why diet still matters.

## Types of Cholesterol

Cholesterol travels through your bloodstream in small packages called lipoproteins. There are two main types:

### LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein) — "Bad" Cholesterol
LDL carries cholesterol FROM the liver TO your cells. When there's too much LDL, it can build up in the walls of your arteries, forming plaque. This buildup narrows arteries and can lead to heart attacks and strokes.

A normal LDL level is below 100 mg/dL. Optimal is below 70 mg/dL for people at high risk.

### HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein) — "Good" Cholesterol
HDL works like a cleanup crew. It picks up excess cholesterol from your arteries and carries it BACK to the liver, where it's broken down and removed from the body. Higher HDL is generally better — you want at least 40 mg/dL for men and 50 mg/dL for women.

### Triglycerides
Triglycerides are another type of fat in your blood. Your body converts excess calories, sugar, and alcohol into triglycerides, which are stored in fat cells. High triglycerides combined with high LDL or low HDL can increase your risk of heart disease. Normal is below 150 mg/dL.

## The Ratio Matters

Doctors increasingly look at the ratio of total cholesterol to HDL, not just individual numbers. A ratio below 5:1 is considered good, and 3.5:1 is optimal. This means if your total cholesterol is 200 and your HDL is 50, your ratio is 4:1 — which is healthy.

## Cholesterol Myths

**Myth: Eating eggs raises your cholesterol dangerously.**
Reality: For most people, dietary cholesterol has a modest effect on blood cholesterol. The liver compensates by producing less when you eat more. Most studies show eating 1-3 eggs per day is safe for healthy people.

**Myth: If your cholesterol is normal, you don't need to worry about heart disease.**
Reality: Cholesterol is just one risk factor. Blood pressure, smoking, diabetes, family history, and inflammation all play roles. Some people have heart attacks with "normal" cholesterol levels.

**Myth: Young people don't need to worry about cholesterol.**
Reality: Atherosclerosis (plaque buildup) begins in childhood and progresses silently for decades. Getting tested by age 20 and every 4-6 years after is recommended.

**Myth: You can feel when your cholesterol is high.**
Reality: High cholesterol has NO symptoms. The only way to know is a blood test. This is why it's called a "silent" risk factor.
